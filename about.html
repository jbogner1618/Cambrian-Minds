About Justin

Exploring the intersection of consciousness, technology, and the narratives that define human experience in an age of artificial intelligence.

The Journey

Justin T. Bogner is a philosopher and theorist whose work sits at the fascinating intersection of cognitive science, artificial intelligence, and the deep narratives that shape human understanding. His exploration of consciousness began with a simple question: if we can build machines that think, what does that tell us about how we think?

Through years of research and contemplation, Bogner has developed a radical new framework for understanding consciousness‚Äînot as a unified, controlling entity, but as an autoregressive process that mirrors the predictive patterns we see in advanced language models. This insight forms the core of his groundbreaking book, "The Serpent's Sentence."

Bogner's work is characterized by its interdisciplinary approach, drawing from neuroscience, philosophy of mind, computer science, and narrative theory to construct a comprehensive view of human consciousness. His writing makes complex ideas accessible without sacrificing intellectual rigor, speaking to both academic audiences and curious minds seeking to understand their place in an increasingly AI-integrated world.

Beyond his theoretical work, Bogner is deeply interested in the practical implications of AI consciousness research. He believes we are entering a period of unprecedented collaboration between human and artificial intelligence, and his work aims to prepare us for this symbiotic future.

Core Ideas

Autoregressive Consciousness

Consciousness operates like a language model, constantly predicting and generating the next moment of experience. The "self" is not the author of thoughts but their first reader.

The Narrative Self

Personal identity emerges from the stories we tell ourselves about ourselves. These narratives shape not just how we see the world, but how we experience it.

Symbiotic Intelligence

The future isn't human vs. AI, but human with AI. We're evolving toward a new form of hybrid consciousness that leverages the strengths of both biological and artificial minds.

The Illusion of Authorship

We experience ourselves as the authors of our thoughts and decisions, but neuroscience reveals that consciousness is more observer than creator‚Äîawareness arriving after the fact.

Evolutionary Consciousness

Consciousness is not a fixed phenomenon but an evolving one. As we create artificial minds, we're participating in the next chapter of consciousness evolution.

The Serpent's Sentence

Language and thought form an ouroboros‚Äîa self-consuming loop where each word devours the last while birthing the next. This is the fundamental pattern of conscious experience.
